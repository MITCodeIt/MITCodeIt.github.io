B. Making the Parrot Rise and Fall

  1. Add an if-else statement and a "space key pressed?" block to your program that makes the sprite move up and down when the user presses and releases the space bar.
  2. Program the if condition to move y by 5, and the else condition to move y by -5.
  3. Add costume changes to make the sprite look more realistic as it flies up and down.

C. Winning and Losing at Cave Surfing

  1. Program a winning condition for the player sprite. (hint: use if statements and color sensing blocks)
  2. Program a losing condition for the player sprite. (hint: the cave walls all have a black border)
  3. Program the game to reset when someone wins or loses. (hint: use a stop-all block)

Add-ons

  • Add a drum beat and background music to your game.
  • Design a new cave sprite with black edges and add it to your game.
  • Make the player press the space bar over and over to keep the sprite in flight.
  • Make the player sprite explode when it touches the walls of the cave.
  • Create additional game levels that get faster and harder as the player progresses. (hint: use a variable to keep track of the level)
  • Make the sprite dive, or move down faster than it falls. (hint: use nested if-else statements)
